AI-Based Protein Complex Prediction with AlphaFold3

BIO512 Structural Biology - Predicting Mutation Effects on Protein Complexes

Author

Sayyed Jalil Mahdizadeh

Published

November 10, 2025

1 Introduction

In this practical we continue working on the Barnase-Barstar protein complex (PDBid: 1BRS) from Bacillus amyloliquefaciens. This time, we will use AlphaFold3 (AF3) to predict the structure of the complex from sequence alone, and assess how well AF3 can predict the effects of specific mutations on the complex structure.

Prerequisites
  • Completed analysis of 1BRS crystal structure from previous practical
  • FASTA sequences of Barnase and Barstar from PDB
  • Gmail account for AlphaFold server access
Learning Objectives

By the end you should be able to:

  • Use AlphaFold3 server for protein complex prediction
  • Interpret AF3 confidence metrics (pTM and ipTM)
  • Design and analyze mutational studies computationally
  • Compare predicted structures with experimental crystal structures
  • Understand limitations of AI-based structure prediction for mutations

2 AlphaFold3 Prediction Setup

2.1 Preparing sequences

You should have obtained the FASTA sequences from the previous practical. If not, go back and do so now.

2.2 Wild-type complex prediction

Access AlphaFold3 server:

  • Navigate to https://alphafoldserver.com
  • Log in with your Gmail account

Submit wild-type prediction:

  • Enter the WT sequence of Barstar (first protein)
  • Add entity as “protein”
  • Enter the WT sequence of Barnase (second protein)
  • Add entity as “protein”
  • Click “Continue and preview job”
  • Give a name to the job: wtBarnase-wtBarstar
  • Submit the job
Processing time

AF3 jobs can take several hours to complete. If the server is busy, it may take a very long, use the precomputed results available on Vera then instead.

Download results: - Once completed, click on the job

Tip

Note the confidence metrics:

  • pTM (global metric): Confidence in individual protein structures
  • ipTM (interface metric): Confidence in protein-protein interface
  • Download results by clicking “Download”
  • Rename the file to wtBarnase_wtBarstar.zip

Extract on the cluster:

# Unzip the results
unzip wtBarnase_wtBarstar.zip -d wtBarnase_wtBarstar

3 Mutational Analysis

Based on the previous structural analysis, we identified that ASP39 and ASP35 of Barstar are critical for binding. We’ll test two different mutation scenarios:

3.1 Mutation to Alanine (conservative)

Prepare D39A-D35A mutant sequences:

  • Take the Barstar sequence from the FASTA file
  • Find position 39 (ASP/D) and replace with Alanine (A)
  • Find position 35 (ASP/D) and replace with Alanine (A)
  • Keep Barnase sequence unchanged

Submit D39A-D35A prediction:

  • Enter the mutated Barstar sequence
  • Enter the WT Barnase sequence
  • Job name: wtBarnase_D39A-D35ABarstar
  • Submit and download results when complete

3.2 Mutation to Arginine (charge reversal)

Prepare D39R-D35R mutant sequences:

  • Take the Barstar sequence from the FASTA file
  • Find position 39 (ASP/D) and replace with Arginine (R)
  • Find position 35 (ASP/D) and replace with Arginine (R)
  • Keep Barnase sequence unchanged

Submit D39R-D35R prediction:

  • Enter the mutated Barstar sequence
  • Enter the WT Barnase sequence
  • Job name: wtBarnase_D39R-D35RBarstar
  • Submit and download results when complete

3.3 Structural comparison with crystal structure

Prepare YASARA for comparison: Clean up the YASARA’s screen (File > New > Yes) and open the prepared crystal structure from step 9 (1BRS_complex.pdb). Also open the “…model_0.cif” structure predicted by AF3 for the WT sequences (drag and drop). You can find it within the folder “wtBarnase_wtBarstar” from step18

AF3 output format

AF3 predicts 5 models (model_0 to model_4) where model_0 always has the highest-ranking confidence value.

For better visualization, it’s better to color each molecule in each complex differently.

Superpose predicted on crystal structure: Now you have two protein complexes on YASARA, crystal structure and AF3 predicted for WT sequence. Now we superpose the predicted structure on crystal structure.

  • Analyze > Superpose > Objects
  • Select “fold_wtbaranase…” (predicted structure) from Sequence panel > OK
  • Select “1BRS_complex…” (crystal structure) from Sequence panel > OK
  • In “Set parameters” window: Uncheck all boxes except “Residue name” and “Residue number” > OK

Calculate RMSD for Barstar: Then we calculate the RMSD value between Barstar proteins by

  • Analyze > RMSD of > Molecules
  • Choose “D” (Barstar of crystal structure) from Sequence panel > OK
  • Choose “A” (predicted Barstar) > OK > OK
  • RMSD value will be shown in the command window

Repeat for mutant structures:

  • Clean up YASARA screen and repeat the superposition and RMSD calculation for both mutant predictions
